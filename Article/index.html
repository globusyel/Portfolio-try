Justice for Kateryna Handziuk

Why those involved in the murder have not yet been punished

The Murder of an Activist: Reasons and Attempts to Prevent It

  • On July 31, 2018, Kateryna Handziuk, a civic activist, was attacked with acid, suffering burns on about 30% of her body.
  • She was in intensive care for 96 days, underwent 14 surgeries and died on November 4, 2018, in a hospital in Kyiv.
  • The attack was allegedly ordered by Vladyslav Manger, former head of the Kherson Regional Council, and organized by his assistant Oleksiy Levin.
  • Manger was implicated due to Handziuk's outspoken criticism of him and the Kherson regional authorities for corruption and environmental crimes, including deforestation and the suspicious forest fire in the Oleshky district.
  • On June 26, 2023, the Dniprovsky District Court of Kyiv sentenced Manger and Levin to 10 years in prison for ordering and organizing the attack. Their lawyers filed an appeal, which began on February 5, 2024.
  • The 10-year statute of limitations on the case expires in July 2028. If a final verdict is not delivered by then, the defendants could avoid punishment for Handziuk's murder.

Information About Activism and the Activist's Influence on Society

  • Kateryna Handziuk was a prominent activist, particularly noted for her role in Euromaidan in Kherson, where she actively protested against pro-Russian demonstrations.
  • She was vocal in her opposition to local corruption, specifically calling out Manger and the regional authorities for their alleged involvement in environmental destruction and illegal activities.
  • Her activism earned her a significant following and support within Ukrainian civil society, highlighting issues of corruption and the need for accountability among public officials.
  • Handziuk’s work exposed deep-rooted issues in Kherson’s political and administrative spheres, making her a target for those threatened by her revelations.

Conclusions and What We Can Do With Them

  • The case of Kateryna Handziuk exemplifies the significant challenges activists face in fighting corruption and injustice within Ukraine.
  • Ensuring timely and effective judicial processes is crucial to uphold justice and deter future attacks on activists.
  • Public awareness and continued pressure from civil society and international communities are essential to hold perpetrators accountable and prevent legal loopholes from being exploited.
  • Strengthening legal protections for activists and implementing reforms to prevent judicial delays can help create a safer environment for those advocating for transparency and human rights.
